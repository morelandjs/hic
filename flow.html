Anisotropic flow¶

The flow module handles anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) and related tasks.

Flows \(v_n\) are the Fourier coefficients of the transverse momentum distribution:

\[\frac{dN}{d\phi} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \biggl[ 1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty 2v_n\cos n(\phi - \Psi_\text{RP}) \biggr],\]

where \(\phi\) is the azimuthal angle of transverse momentum, \(n\) is the order of anisotropy, and \(\Psi_\text{RP}\) is the reaction plane angle. The coefficients are

\[v_n = \langle \cos n(\phi - \Psi_\text{RP}) \rangle\]

where the average is over all particles and events.

The flow vector¶

The complex flow vector for an event is

\[Q_n = \sum_{i=1}^M e^{in\phi_i}.\]

Flow vectors can be calculated in hic with hic.flow.qn. First, let’s generate some random angles:

import numpy as np
phi = np.random.uniform(-np.pi, np.pi, 100)

And now calculate \(Q_2\):

from hic import flow
q2 = flow.qn(2, phi)

We can also do multiple \(Q_n\) at once:

q2, q3, q4 = flow.qn((2, 3, 4), phi)

Flow cumulants¶

Multi-particle flow cumulants are implemented in the class hic.flow.FlowCumulant using the direct Q-cumulant method from Bilandzic (2010).

Reference¶

hic.flow.qn(n, phi)¶

Calculate the complex flow vector \(Q_n\).

Parameters:
  • n (int or iterable of ints) – Harmonic(s) \(n\) to calculate.
  • phi (array-like) – Azimuthal angles.
Returns:

A single complex number if only one n was given or a complex array for multiple n.
hic.flow.flow_pdf(phi, vn=None, psin=0)¶

Evaluate the flow probability density function \(dN/d\phi\).

Parameters:
  • phi (float or array-like) – Azimuthal angle(s) \(\phi\).
  • vn (float or iterable of floats) – (optional) Flow coefficients \(v_n\), starting with \(v_2\).
  • psin (float or iterable of floats) – (optional) Event plane angles \(\Psi_n\) matching vn.
Returns:

The flow PDF evaluated at phi.
hic.flow.sample_flow_pdf(multiplicity, vn=None, psin=0)¶

Randomly sample azimuthal angles \(\phi\) with specified flows.

Parameters:
  • multiplicity (int) – Number to sample.
  • vn (float or iterable of floats) – (optional) Flow coefficients \(v_n\), starting with \(v_2\). If not given, uniformly sample angles from \([-\pi, \pi)\).
  • psin (float or iterable of floats) – (optional) Event plane angles \(\Psi_n\) matching vn.
Returns:

Array of angles.
class hic.flow.FlowCumulant(multiplicities, qn)¶

Multi-particle flow correlations and cumulants for an ensemble of events.

Parameters:
  • multiplicities (array-like) – Event-by-event multiplicities.
  • qn (dict or iterable of pairs) – Event-by-event \(Q_n\) vectors, either a dict of the form {n: qn} or an iterable of pairs (n, qn), where each n is an integer and each qn is an array of event-by-event flow vectors.

Parameters:

  • k (int) – Correlation order.
cumulant(n, k, error=False)¶

Calculate \(c_n\{k\}\), the \(k\)-particle cumulant for \(n\)th-order anisotropy.

Parameters:
  • n (int) – Flow order.
  • k (int) – Correlation order.
flow(n, k, error=False, imaginary='nan')¶

Calculate \(v_n\{k\}\), the estimate of flow coefficient \(v_n\) from the \(k\)-particle cumulant.

Parameters:
  • n (int) – Flow order.
  • k (int) – Correlation order.
  • imaginary (str) –

    (optional) Determines behavior when the computed flow is imaginary:

    • 'nan' (default) – Return NaN and raise a RuntimeWarning.
    • 'negative' – Return the negative absolute value.
    • 'zero' – Return 0.0.
